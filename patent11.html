Electrode Coating Material Screening Method, Electrode Material, and Secondary Battery

Purpose:

This patent introduces a method for screening electrode coating materials, aimed at improving the electrochemical performance and stability of electrode active materials in secondary batteries, such as lithium-ion batteries.

Principle:

The method involves several steps using density functional theory (DFT) calculations to identify the best coating materials for electrodes:

Determine the Interface Structure of Electrode Active Materials:

  • Calculate the surface energy of different crystal faces of the electrode active materials using DFT.
  • Identify the interface structure based on the stability of the surface energy.

Determine the Interface System of Coating Materials:

  • Calculate the surface energy of different coating materials using DFT.
  • Identify the coating materials' interface system based on their surface energy stability.

Forming Energy System of Coating Materials:

  • Calculate the formation energy between the electrode active materials and the coating materials to determine their compatibility.
  • Use the formation energy to create a database of stable coating materials.

Migration System of Coating Materials:

  • Calculate the lithium-ion migration rate within the coating materials using DFT.
  • Identify materials with high migration efficiency and low energy barriers for lithium-ion transport.

Reaction Kinetics System of Coating Materials:

  • Calculate the reaction barriers and thermodynamics between the coating materials and the electrolyte using DFT.
  • Identify materials with low reactivity to the electrolyte, ensuring stability during battery operation.

Screening of Electrode Coating Materials:

  • Select coating materials that meet both high migration efficiency and low electrolyte reactivity criteria to ensure optimal electrochemical performance.

Advantages:

Precision Screening:

Uses high-throughput DFT calculations to quickly and accurately screen the best coating materials at the microstructural level.

Enhanced Electrochemical Performance:

The selected coating materials improve the stability and performance of the electrode active materials by preventing side reactions and improving lithium-ion transport.

Comprehensive Database:

Establishes a database of stable and efficient coating materials, facilitating the development of high-performance secondary batteries.

Applications:

Electrode Material:

The method produces electrode materials with core-shell structures, where the core is the electrode active material, and the shell is the screened coating material. This structure enhances the overall capacity and stability of the electrodes.

Secondary Battery:

The improved electrode materials are used to manufacture secondary batteries with higher energy density, better cycle stability, and longer lifespan.

Key Processes:

  • Surface Energy Calculation: Determines the stability of various crystal faces and selects the most stable ones for coating.
  • Formation Energy Calculation: Identifies compatible and stable combinations of electrode and coating materials.
  • Lithium-Ion Migration and Reaction Kinetics: Ensures the selected coatings facilitate efficient lithium-ion transport and have low reactivity with the electrolyte.

This method provides a systematic and efficient approach to enhancing the performance of electrode materials in secondary batteries, supporting the development of advanced energy storage solutions.
